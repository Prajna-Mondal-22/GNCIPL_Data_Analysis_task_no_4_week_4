# GNCIPL_Data_Analysis_task_no_4_week_4

This analysis explores global population patterns using data from the World Bank, UN datasets, and national statistics. A structured data model combining Country, Year, and Population Fact tables enables consistent examination of trends across regions, income groups, and time periods.
The results show that population growth is unevenly distributed across the world. Asia remains the most populated region, while Africa exhibits the fastest growth rates, indicating it will be the primary contributor to global population increase in the coming years. In contrast, Europe and North America show low or stagnant growth, driven by aging populations and low fertility rates.
Urbanization is a defining factor shaping demographic trends. Many countries are becoming increasingly urban, with Latin America and North America already exceeding 80% urban population, while South Asia and Africa continue transitioning from rural-based societies. This urban shift affects housing, infrastructure, employment, and resource distribution.
